Group trip planner FAQ

How is Ableten different from Wanderlog or other trip planner apps?

Wanderlog excels at building itineraries from scratch inside a travel app. Ableten starts upstream: collecting messy recs from friends (links, screenshots, chats), organizing them into collections, then composing a plan grounded in those sources. It's research → itinerary, not blank-slate → itinerary.

Can friends add restaurant and place recommendations to my trip?

Yes. Use Collect Recs to send a link where friends submit places they love. Each submission saves with links and context into your trip collection, ready for the itinerary board.

Can we share and edit a group travel itinerary together?

You can share an itinerary draft via link today. Collaborative commenting, logistics constraints (time and distance), and join-for-part-of-trip planning are in development—email us for early access.

Can I save Google Maps links and screenshots from group chats?

Yes. Forward images and links to Ableten's email capture, or save places while browsing with the Chrome extension. Map links and notes are extracted and organized automatically.

Does Ableten work for partial trips—when not everyone joins the whole time?

That's on the roadmap: mark who joins which days and keep a single shared itinerary. Join the early access list to help shape it.

Is Ableten free for trip planning?

Ableten offers a free Chrome extension and web app. Sign up at app.ableten.xyz to build trip collections and generate itinerary drafts. Questions? Visit our support center.
